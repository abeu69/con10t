The drawings

Part of the material collected by Friedrich W. Hinkel during his long-lasting research endeavors in the Sudan are the over 4.000 (mostly hand-drawn) drawings, which illustrate all aspects of the archaeological heritage of the Sudan. Apart from architectural drawings and copies of older illustrations made by Hinkel the archive also contains hand-drawn map sheets for the “The Archaeological Map of the Sudan“ as well as illustrations of archaeological objects. Some drawing, such as illustrations of the reliefs of the pyramids of Meroe, were made by draftsmen hired by Dr. Hinkel according to his specifications.

In the course of digitization all drawings were registered and their subject matter identified. Drawings showing the same or similar content, such as the so called pattern books featuring all pyramids of Meroe, were integrated into the iDAI.Bookbrowser, while single sheets were structured geographically according to the AMS-system and entered into iDAI.objects. The connection between iDAI.objects and the iDAI.gazetteer allows for a systematic search for information regarding specific sites and buildings.

The copyright for all drawings in the archive of Friedrich W. Hinkel rests (unless stated otherwise) with the DAI.
